## Research Direction
Test whether longer-term individual learning trajectories (beyond 4 hours) under island test conditions can achieve expert-level Oldowan knapping performance

## Core Hypothesis
The hypothesis predicts that technique-naïve humans can individually reinnovate early knapping techniques and produce functional cutting tools without cultural transmission of know-how, supporting the alternative hypothesis over the cultural transmission necessity hypothesis.

## Methodology Template
- **Design**: Empirical analysis guided by: Do early knapping techniques require cultural transmission of know-how, or can they be individually reinnovated in cultural isolation?
