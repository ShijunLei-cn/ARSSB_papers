## Research Direction
Collect data on long-run inflation expectations to identify parameters of the heterogeneous priors model.

## Core Hypothesis
The basic noisy-information model predicts: (1) mean higher-order expectation close to mean first-order; (2) perfect cross-sectional correlation between first- and higher-order beliefs; (3) lower dispersion in higher-order than first-order beliefs; (4) lower uncertainty in higher-order than first-order beliefs; (5) equality of uncertainty and disagreement; (6) stronger response to first-order signal than higher-order signal.

## Methodology Template
- **Design**: Empirical analysis guided by: What are the empirical properties of firms' higher-order macroeconomic expectations, and how well do noisy-information models with infinite regress ex
- **Key Variables**: First-order inflation expectations (point and distributional), higher-order inflation expectations, uncertainty (standard deviation of probability distribution), disagreement (cross-sectional standard deviation), firm characteristics (size, age, exports), manager characteristics (tenure, education, gender), level-k thinking (beauty contest guess), responses to information treatments.
- **Suggested Data Sources**: Survey of firms in New Zealand, Kompass New Zealand (KNZ) and Equifax (EQ) for sampling frame.
